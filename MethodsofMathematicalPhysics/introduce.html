课程介绍


       本课程包括两部分内容：复变函数与数学物理方程。复变函数是微积分的内容从实变量到复变量的扩展。它是进一步学习数学物理方程和特殊函数的基础。其本身在物理、力学和工程问题中也具有多方面的应用。数学物理方程主要研究物理或工程问题中所涉及的各种偏微分方程和积分方程。本课程只研究偏微分方程，主要是二阶线性偏微分方程，包括波动方程、输运方程和稳定场方程。在偏微分方程的导出、求解和对解的物理分析三个方面，求解是本课程的重点内容。本课程所涉及的方法在经典物理、近代物理和工程技术中都有广泛的应用。
内容包括：解析函数的基本性质；Cauchy积分定理；Cauchy积分公式；解析函数的Taylor展开式和Laurent展开式；留数定理及其在积分计算中的应用；数理方程的导出；定解条件；分离变量法及其推广；Fourier 变换法；行波法；Delta函数；球函数及其应用；柱函数及其应用；Green 函数法等。
数理方程的计算比较繁琐，需要较多练习才能掌握好。

This course includes two parts: Functions of a complex variable and mathematical physics equations. The first part is the extension of calculus to complex variables. It is a prerequisite for studying mathematical physics equations and special functions. It is also widely applicable to problems of physics, mechanics and engineering. Mathematical physics equations include various partial differential equations and integral equations arising from physical and engineering problems. The course only deals with partial differential equations, mainly linear ones of second order. These consist of wave equations, heat conduction equations and time-independent equations. Among the three subjects of derivation of mathematical physics equations, approaches to their solutions, and physical explanation of the solutions, we emphasize on the various approaches to solutions. The methods presented in this course are widely applicable in classical physics, quantum physics and engineering.
The course covers the following topics: Basic properties of analytic functions; Cauchy integral theorem; Cauchy integral formula; Taylor expansion and Laurent expansion of analytic funcitons; Residue theorem and its application to the calculations of various integrals; Derivation of mathematical physics equations; Initial and boundary conditions; Method of separation of variables and its extension; Fourier transform; Traveling wave method; Delta function; Spherical functions and their applications; Cylindrical functions and their applications; Method of Green functions.
The calculations in the part of mathematical physics equations are tedious. In order to master the methods and techniques, a considerable amount of exercises are necessary.
