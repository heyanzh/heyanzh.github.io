教学大纲


第一部份　量子力学（36学时）

第一章 绪论（4学时）
1.1 经典物理学的困难
1.2 光的波粒二象性
1.3 原子结构的玻尔理论
1.4 微粒的波粒二象性
重点：经典物理学的困难，量子力学的实验基础，波粒二象性，量子化
难点：波粒二象性

第二章 波函数和薛定谔方程（12学时）
2.1 波函数的统计解释
2.2 态迭加原理
2.3 薛定谔方程
2.4 粒子流密度和粒子数守恒定律
2.5 定态薛定谔方程
2.6 一维无限深势阱
2.7 线形谐振子
2.8 势垒贯穿
重点：波函数统计解释，态叠加原理，波函数的几率幅解释，束缚态与分立谱，一维定态的能量本征求解，势垒贯穿的透射系数
难点：波函数统计解释，态叠加原理、定态薛定谔方程的求解

第三章 量子力学中的力学量（12学时）
3.1 表示力学量的算符
3.2 动量算符和角动量算符
3.3 电子在库仑场中的运动
3.4 氢原子
3.5 厄密算符本征函数的正交性
3.6 算符与力学量的关系
3.7 算符的对易关系
3.8 力学量平均值随时间的变化
重点：量子力学的基本假定，量子力学算符与厄密算符，动量算符和角动量算符，厄密算符本征函数的正交性，中心力场下的量子力学系统的一般性质，类氢原子的能级，算符的对易关系
难点：角动量算符的本征函数

第七章 自旋与全同粒子（7学时）
7.1 电子自旋
7.2 电子的自旋算符和自旋函数
7.3 简单塞曼效应
7.6 全同粒子的特性
7.7 全同粒子体系的波函数
7.8 两个电子的自旋函数
7.9 氦原子
重点：自旋、自旋算符和自旋函数，简单塞曼效应，全同粒子的特性及其波函数
难点：自旋概念，全同粒子的特性及其波函数

第二部份 热力学与统计物理 (36学时)

第六章 近独立粒子的最概然分布（8学时）
6.1 粒子运动状态的经典描述
6.2 粒子运动状态的量子描述
6.3 系统微观运动状态的描述
6.4 等概率原理
6.5 分布和微观状态
6.6 玻尔兹曼分布
6.7 玻色分布和费米分布
6.8 三种分布及其关系
重点：粒子运动状态、u空间、微观运动状态的概念；粒子运动状态的经典描述和量子描述；粒子的状态与u空间体积元的对应关系；系统微观运动状态的描述
难点：微观运动状态、系统微观运动状态、三种分布及其关系

第七章 玻耳兹曼统计理论（8学时）
7.1 热力学量的统计表达式
7.2 理想气体的物态方程
7.3 麦克斯韦速度分布律
7.4 能量均分定理
7.5 理想气体的内能和热容量
7.6 理想气体的熵
重点：分布和微观状态、玻耳兹曼分布、热力学公式、玻耳兹曼关系、固体的热容量的概念；理想气体的热力学函数；麦克斯韦速度分布律；能量均分定理及其应用
难点：玻耳兹曼分布下的热力学公式、麦克斯韦速度分布律

第八章 玻色统计与费密统计理论（6学时）
8.1 热力学量的统计表达式
8.2 弱简并理想玻色气体和费米气体
8.3 玻色—爱因斯坦凝聚
8.4 光子气体
8.5 金属中的自由电子气体
重点：玻色分布、费密分布、光子气体的概念；玻色分布和费密分布的特点；玻色—爱因斯坦凝聚；理解和掌握金属中的自由电子的热力学特性
难点：玻色/费密分布下的热力学公式、玻色/费密分布的特点、玻色—爱因斯坦凝聚

Chapter 1 Introduction（4 class hours）

1.2 Wave-particle duality of light
1.3 Bohr theory of atomic structure
1.4 Wave-particle duality of microscopic particle

Chapter 2 Wave function and Schrodinger equation （12 class hours）
2.1 Statistical exposition of wave function
2.2 Superposition Principle of States
2.3 Schrodinger equation
2.4 Particle flux density and Law of conservation of particle number
2.5 Time-independent Schrodinger equation
2.6 One dimensional infinite potential well
2.7 Harmonic oscillator
2.8 Potential barriers
Key: Statistical exposition of wave function, Superposition Principle of States, Interpretation of probability amplitude interpretation wave function, Bound state and discrete spectrum, Solving eigenenergy of one dimensional Stationary States, Transmission coefficient of potential barriers
Difficulty: Statistical exposition of wave function, Superposition Principle of States, Solving eigenenergy of one dimensional Stationary States

Chapter 3 Observable quantity of quantum mechanics（12 class hours）
3.1 Observable operator
3.2 Momentum operator and Angular momentum operator
3.3 Motion of electron under Coulomb field
3.4 Hydrogen atom
3.5 Orthogonality of eigenfunction of hermitian operator
3.6 Relation between observable operator and quantity
3.7 Commutation relation of operator
3.8 Change of mean value of observable quantity with time
Key: Basic assumptions of quantum mechanics, observable operator and hermitian operator, Momentum operator and Angular momentum operator, Orthogonality of eigenfunction of hermitian operator, General properties of quantum systems under the central force field, Energy levels of hydrogen like atoms, Commutation relation of operator
Difficulty: Eigenfunction of angular momentum operator

Chapter 7 Spins and identical particles （7 class hours）
7.1 Electron spin
7.2 Operator of electron spin and spin function
7.3 Simple Zeeman effect
7.6 Properties of identical particles
7.7 Wave function of identical particles
7.8 Spin function of two electrons
7.9 Helium atoms
Key: Spin, Spin operator, Spin function, Simple Zeeman effect, Properties and wave function of identical particles
Difficulty: spin, Properties and wave function of identical particles

Part II Thermodynamics and statistical mechanics (36 class hours)

Chapter 6 Most probability distribution of sub-independent particles（8 class hours）
6.1 Classical description of particle motion
6.2 Quantum description of particle motion
6.3 Description of microscopic motion of the system
6.4 Equal probability principle
6.5 Distribution and microscopic state
6.6 Boltzmann distribution
6.7 Bose and Fermi distribution
6.8 Three distributions and their relations
Key: Particles motion, u-space, Micro motion state, Classical description and Quantum description of particle motion, Relations between particle state and volume element of u-space, Description of microscopic motion of the system
Difficulty: Micro motion state, Microscopic motion of the system, Three distributions and their relations

Chapter 7 Boltzmann statistical theory（8 class hours）
7.1 Statistical representation of thermodynamic variables
7.2 Equation of state of ideal gas
7.3 Maxwell's law of distribution of speed
7.4 Equipartition theorem
7.5 Internal energy and heat capacity of ideal gas
7.6 Entropy of ideal gas
Key: Distribution and microscopic state, Boltzmann distribution, Thermodynamics equations, Boltzmann relation, Heat capacity of solids, Thermodynamic function of ideal gas, Maxwell's law of distribution of speed, Equipartition theorem and application
Difficulty: Thermodynamic equations under Boltzmann distribution, Maxwell's law of distribution of speed

Chapter 8 Bose and Fermi statistics（6 class hours）
8.1 Statistical representation of thermodynamic variables
8.2 Weak degenerate ideal Bose gas and Fermi gas
8.3 Bose-einstein condensation
8.4 Photon gas
8.5 Free electron gas in metals
Key: Bose and Fermi distribution, Photon gas, Characteristics of Bose and Fermi distribution, Bose-einstein condensation, Thermodynamic properties of free electron gas in metals
Difficulty: Thermodynamic equations under Bose and Fermi distribution, Characteristics of Bose and Fermi distribution, Bose-einstein condensation


环节安排：
本课程共20周（每周4节，其中授课占18周，复习和期末考试占2周）。教学过程中，利用多媒体设备，提高学生积极性的同时，建立学生良好的物理图像。每周布置约八道习题，培养学生解决物理问题的能力，巩固所学的内容。教学内容之进度和安排，会根据实际教学情况，作适当小范围的调整。
There are 20 weeks for this course (4 class hours in a week, 18 weeks for in-classroom instruction, 2 weeks for revision and final examination). Through classes, we will take the advantage of multimedia to demonstrate the contents of courses. Not only can it raise students’ enthusiastic, but also is good for building up physics pictures. We will arrange about 8 questions every week, to cultivate their physical problem-solving ability and consolidate their knowledge. Besides, the schedule and arrangement of the courses will be slightly adjust according to the practical teaching situation.

教学方法：
课堂讲授为主，隔几周安排一次习题讲解。
In-classroom instruction is the principal teaching methodology; there will be an explanation of the exercises every several weeks.
